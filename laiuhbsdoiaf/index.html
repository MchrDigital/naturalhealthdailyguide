Our beloved fur babies can die from heat stroke in as little as 15 minutes.

Dogs normally have 102°. Just 4° more (106°) leads to heat stroke. 50% of cases end fatally. [1]

And trust me, keeping your dog cool and comfortable in hot weather is key. Let me show you a simple way to make that happen.

Keep in mind: water, shade, and rest are not enough.

Even when the worst-case scenario is avoided, heat stroke can still cause seizures, coma, and permanent damage to a dog’s organs — including brain swelling, kidney failure, and liver damage.

Many people make the dangerous mistake of using cooling gel mats

Even though manufacturers claim they’re “non-toxic” and “bite-resistant”, many dogs still manage to chew through the surface and ingest the gel inside.

There are many reported cases of dogs experiencing symptoms like vomiting, ataxia (loss of coordination), tremors, and even neurological issues affecting the forebrain and cerebellum.

And remember, dogs can’t fully relax on them — there's no support for their neck and head.
