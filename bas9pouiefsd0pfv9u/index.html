Maria Shriver Breaks Her Silence on 60 Minutes and Reveals Why She Believes Ted Turner's Alzheimer's and Her Father's Death May Have Been Preventable

By Lesley Stahl

Maria Shriver believed Ted Turner was receiving the very best care money could buy.

The best doctors.

The best hospitals.

Two million acres in Montana and a fortune that could fund anything.

She believed the same about her father, Sargent Shriver, years before.

And yet...

Nothing stopped her from watching both men slowly disappear before her eyes.

When Maria finally agreed to speak about it on 60 Minutes, the entire studio fell silent.

"I lost them both the same way."
"First... little by little."
"...and then all at once."
"Ted had every resource on the planet. My father had every connection in Washington. And they both died the same death."
"A death that didn't have to happen."

After Ted's passing, Maria didn't grieve quietly. She went to war.

She took the research Ted had funded on his Montana ranch and the data her partner

Dr. Rudolph Tanzi at Harvard had been building for years and demanded one answer. What if Alzheimer's was never caused by aging in the first place?

What they uncovered changed everything.

According to Maria...

The brain wasn't getting old.

It was starving.

Lesley Stahl: "Maria... what made you believe the medical community may have been looking in the wrong direction?"

Maria Shriver: "Ted followed every recommendation. So did my father."

"They both received the best care available."

"And they both kept getting worse."

"That's when I realized the entire medical establishment might have been fighting the wrong enemy all along."

Lesley Stahl: "So you're saying Alzheimer's may not be a natural part of aging?"

Maria Shriver: "That's exactly what we discovered."

"There's a silent process — a toxin called glyphosate that's in our food, our water, our coffee that crosses into the brain and destroys insulin sensitivity in the neurons. It begins years before the first signs of memory loss."

"Today, researchers refer to it as Brain Diabetes."

Lesley Stahl: "Are you saying the brain can actually develop a form of diabetes without a person even knowing it?"

Maria Shriver: "Yes."

"Without insulin, the neurons can't absorb glucose. They starve. That's what killed my father. That's what killed Ted"

"And once we understood that mechanism... the conventional theory of Alzheimer's completely fell apart."

Lesley Stahl: "Is that what led your team to Ted Turner's ranch in Montana?"

Maria Shriver: "Yes."

"Ted had turned that ranch into a laboratory before he died. His team found that a raw honey from the Sardinia region could remove the toxin from the brain. And Dr. Tanzi at Harvard found the second half a compound in Japanese coffee called trigonelline that restores insulin sensitivity."

"Together, they reverse the damage. Ted had both pieces growing on that ranch. Meters apart. But his disease had already progressed too far."

Lesley Stahl: "And did you find out what it was?"

Maria Shriver: "We discovered why so many treatments fail."

"We discovered what was really happening inside the brain."

"And we discovered why millions of families including mine, including Ted's were given the wrong answers for decades."

Lesley Stahl: "Can people learn about this discovery?"

Maria Shriver: "Absolutely."

"We've prepared a special presentation that walks you through the entire investigation."

"Why the brain begins losing energy years before a diagnosis."

"What Ted found. What Dr. Tanzi proved. And what we gave to the FDA."

"And what we found that completely changed the way we understand Alzheimer's."

"If you're afraid of losing your memories... or watching someone you love slowly fade away the way I watched my father and Ted... I hope you'll watch this while it's still available."
